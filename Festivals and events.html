Festivals & Events of Bangladesh

Explore the Vibrant Festivals & Events

In this page, you will learn about the major festivals and events celebrated in Bangladesh. From cultural and religious festivals to national commemorations, each event reflects the country's rich heritage and traditions. Discover how people come together to celebrate these special occasions with joy and enthusiasm.

Pohela Boishakh

Pohela Boishakh, the Bengali New Year, is celebrated on April 14 with great enthusiasm. The day starts with "Mangal Shobhajatra," a UNESCO-recognized procession featuring colorful masks and banners. People dress in traditional attire, with women wearing red and white sarees and men in panjabis. Fairs and cultural programs take place across the country, featuring folk music, dance, and poetry. Special foods such as panta bhat (soaked rice) with fried hilsa fish are enjoyed by many. Businesses open new accounting books in a ceremony called "Halkhata." Families visit loved ones, and streets are decorated with vibrant artworks. This festival symbolizes unity, culture, and the spirit of Bengali identity.

Eid-ul-Fitr & Eid-ul-Adha

Eid-ul-Fitr marks the end of Ramadan, the holy month of fasting. On this day, Muslims perform special prayers at mosques and open grounds. Families gather to exchange greetings and gifts, and children receive "Eidi" money from elders. Traditional dishes such as semai, biryani, and kebabs are enjoyed. Eid-ul-Adha, known as the Festival of Sacrifice, commemorates Prophet Ibrahim’s devotion to God. People sacrifice animals such as cows, goats, or camels, and the meat is shared with family, friends, and the poor. Both Eid festivals emphasize generosity, unity, and the importance of community bonds.

Durga Puja

Durga Puja is the most significant Hindu festival in Bangladesh, celebrated with grand decorations and rituals. The festival honors Goddess Durga’s victory over the demon Mahishasura, symbolizing the triumph of good over evil. Temples and pandals are beautifully decorated with lights and artistic idols of the goddess. Devotees perform daily prayers, rituals, and cultural programs, including dance and drama performances. The festival lasts for five days, ending with the immersion of Durga’s idol in rivers. Families come together to share festive meals and sweets. People dress in vibrant traditional clothes and visit different pandals to enjoy the celebrations. The festival fosters unity and cultural harmony among different communities.

Boshonto Utshob

Boshonto Utshob, the Spring Festival, is a colorful celebration marking the arrival of spring in Bangladesh. People dress in yellow and red to symbolize warmth and energy. Open-air concerts, poetry recitations, and traditional dances are performed in schools and cultural centers. The festival is associated with joy, love, and the renewal of nature. Women wear floral garlands, and men wear traditional panjabis, adding to the festive spirit. The event is particularly grand in Dhaka, especially at the Faculty of Fine Arts, Dhaka University. Artists paint beautiful alpanas (traditional floor art), and people exchange sweets and flowers. This festival unites people in a celebration of beauty and tradition.

Independence Day

Celebrated on March 26, Independence Day marks Bangladesh's liberation from Pakistan in 1971. The day starts with the raising of the national flag and tributes at the National Martyrs’ Memorial. Schools, colleges, and offices organize parades, cultural performances, and discussions on the history of independence. The streets are decorated with lights, banners, and the colors of the national flag. TV channels broadcast patriotic songs, documentaries, and special programs. Fireworks and candlelight vigils take place in different parts of the country. People pay homage to the freedom fighters, and there is a deep sense of national pride. The day reminds everyone of the sacrifices made for the country’s sovereignty.

Victory Day

Observed on December 16, Victory Day marks the surrender of Pakistani forces in 1971, securing Bangladesh's independence. The day begins with wreath-laying at the National Martyrs’ Memorial. Parades and cultural programs are held nationwide, with patriotic songs and speeches honoring the war heroes. The entire country is decorated with red and green, symbolizing the national flag. TV and radio stations air documentaries and films about the Liberation War. Schools and universities conduct discussions on Bangladesh’s history and independence. Fireworks and candlelight vigils are common in cities and towns. The spirit of freedom and resilience is celebrated, reinforcing national unity and pride.
