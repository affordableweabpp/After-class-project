Top 12 Tourist Spots in Bangladesh

Sundarbans
The Sundarbans is the largest mangrove forest in the world and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. It is famous for its rich biodiversity, including the Bengal tiger.
Cox's Bazar
Known for having the longest unbroken sandy beach in the world, Cox's Bazar is a top tourist destination in Bangladesh, offering breathtaking views and relaxing seaside activities.
Srimangal
Famous for its tea gardens and scenic landscapes, Srimangal is often referred to as the "Tea Capital of Bangladesh."
Paharpur
Paharpur is an ancient Buddhist Vihara, listed as a UNESCO World Heritage Site. It's a historical treasure with ruins dating back to the 8th century.
Rangamati
Rangamati is a picturesque hill district known for its beautiful lakes and tribal culture. The Kaptai Lake is a must-see attraction.
Sylhet
Sylhet is famous for its lush green tea gardens, hills, and the famous shrine of Hazrat Shah Jalal. Itâ€™s a spiritual and scenic retreat.
Bandarbans
Bandarbans is known for its diverse wildlife and hill tracks. It offers trekking opportunities and stunning waterfalls like Nafakhum and Remakri.
Chittagong Hill Tracts
A perfect getaway for nature lovers, with sprawling hills, waterfalls, and serene lakes, Chittagong offers an offbeat adventure.
Kuakata
Known as the "Daughter of the Sea," Kuakata is a unique beach destination where you can enjoy both sunrise and sunset over the Bay of Bengal.
Saint Martin's Island
Saint Martin's Island is a tropical paradise with clear blue waters and coral reefs, offering snorkeling and a laid-back beach experience.
Sonargaon
Sonargaon, located near Dhaka, is a historic city that was once the capital of Bengal. It features the famous Panam City, the Folk Art and Craft Museum, and various ancient structures.
Sixty Dome Mosque
The Sixty Dome Mosque, located in Bagerhat, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site known for its impressive architectural design, featuring 60 domes and a rich Islamic history.
